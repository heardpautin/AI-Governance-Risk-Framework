# AI Acceptable Use & Governance Policy
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** [Insert Date]  
**Policy Owner:** Director of IT  
**Approved By:** CISO  
**Review Cycle:** Annual  

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes governance standards for the responsible, secure, and compliant use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) systems within the organization. The objective is to ensure AI technologies are implemented in alignment with regulatory requirements, industry standards, and enterprise risk management practices.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees, contractors, and third-party users  
- All AI systems owned, managed, or approved by the organization  
- AI systems hosted on-premises, in cloud environments, or via third-party SaaS providers  
- Generative AI, predictive machine learning models, classification systems, and AI-assisted development tools  

---

## 3. AI System Inventory Requirement

All AI systems must be documented in the official AI System Inventory prior to production deployment.

Each documented system must include:

- System Name  
- Business Owner  
- Technical Owner  
- Data Classification  
- Hosting Location  
- Risk Level  
- Regulatory Impact  
- Access Review Frequency  

Unregistered or unapproved AI systems are prohibited.

---

## 4. Data Handling Requirements

AI systems must comply with enterprise data classification standards:

**Public Data**  
Permitted where business appropriate.

**Internal Data**  
Permitted only within approved enterprise AI systems.

**Confidential Data**  
Restricted to approved systems with logging enabled and access controls enforced.

**PII / PHI**  
May only be processed in systems classified as High Risk with documented safeguards and monitoring.

**Source Code / Intellectual Property**  
Restricted to approved development environments with access oversight.

Employees are prohibited from entering regulated or proprietary data into public or unapproved AI tools.

---

## 5. Access Control & Review

AI system access must adhere to the principle of least privilege.

- Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) is required.  
- Privileged access must be formally approved by the Business Owner.  
- High-Risk systems require **Quarterly** access reviews.  
- Medium-Risk systems require **Semi-Annual** access reviews.  
- Low-Risk systems require **Annual** access reviews.  

All access reviews must be documented and retained for audit purposes.

---

## 6. Logging & Monitoring

AI systems must enable logging where technically feasible.

Logging should capture:

- User identity  
- Date and time of access  
- Administrative actions  
- Model configuration or deployment changes  

Monitoring must align with enterprise security logging standards.

---

## 7. Model Governance (Where Applicable)

For predictive or generative AI systems:

- Model performance must be periodically validated.  
- Significant model changes must be documented.  
- Business Owners are responsible for validating output accuracy.  
- High-impact systems (e.g., financial reporting or regulatory exposure) require documented validation at least quarterly.  

---

## 8. Third-Party AI Vendors

Third-party AI platforms must undergo:

- Security review  
- Data processing agreement review  
- Regulatory impact assessment  
- Vendor risk evaluation  

Vendors must align with applicable regulatory requirements (e.g., SOX, HIPAA, privacy standards).

---

## 9. Incident Response

AI-related incidents include:

- Unauthorized data disclosure  
- Model misuse or manipulation  
- Inaccurate outputs causing operational or regulatory impact  
- Access control failures  

Incidents must be reported to the Information Security team within 24 hours.

---

## 10. Regulatory Alignment

This policy aligns with:

- NIST Cybersecurity Framework  
- ISO 27001  
- SOX (where applicable)  
- HIPAA (where applicable)  
- Enterprise Risk Management standards  

---

## 11. Enforcement

Failure to comply with this policy may result in:

- Revocation of system access  
- Disciplinary action  
- Escalation to executive leadership  

---

## 12. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed:

- Annually  
- Upon introduction of new AI systems  
- Following significant regulatory or compliance changes  
- After major AI-related incidents
